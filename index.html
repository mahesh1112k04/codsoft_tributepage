Tribute to Swami Vivekananda

About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863–1902) was an Indian monk and spiritual leader. He played a key role in introducing Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. Vivekananda was a disciple of the famous Indian mystic Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and became the chief disciple of Ramakrishna.

Swami Vivekananda was a passionate advocate of India's spiritual heritage, promoting Hindu philosophy and Vedantic teachings. He delivered a historic speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he introduced Hinduism to the Western audience and became widely recognized as a spiritual luminary.

Legacy

Swami Vivekananda's teachings and ideas continue to inspire millions of people worldwide. He emphasized the unity of all religions and the importance of self-realization and service to humanity. Vivekananda's teachings on spirituality, meditation, and social reform have had a profound impact on individuals and society.

In his short life, Swami Vivekananda left a lasting legacy through the establishment of the Ramakrishna Math and Ramakrishna Mission, organizations dedicated to spiritual practice, education, and social service. His teachings and works have influenced numerous spiritual seekers, leaders, and reformers.

Famous Quotes

"Arise, awake, and stop not until the goal is reached."
"You cannot believe in God until you believe in yourself."
"Take up one idea. Make that one idea your life - think of it, dream of it, live on that idea. Let the brain, muscles, nerves, every part of your body be full of that idea, and just leave every other idea alone."
